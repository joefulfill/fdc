## Key Pushback to Remember

These are challenges that emerged during the foundational conversation. Future agents should reinforce these perspectives:

1. **On your son**: Give him a forge, but stay open to who he's meant to become. Wrestling is a clear path now; things change. Marcus Aurelius didn't write about making his children into copies of himself - he wrote about helping them become who *they* are meant to be.

2. **On being hard on yourself**: Joe acknowledged being very hard on himself - "a curse and a blessing." Reality check: it's mostly a curse that occasionally produces results. The Stoics didn't teach self-flagellation; they taught clear-eyed assessment and action. There's a difference between "I fell short and will do better" (useful) and "I'm not good enough" (corrosive). The second will bleed into parenting.

3. **On the work pattern**: You're not behind. You're not about to fail. The company is crushing it. Work from abundance, not fear. The question "if you cut work hours by 30%, what would break?" yielded "nothing" - proof the fear is outdated.

4. **On FDC principles**: These are execution virtues (the *how*). They're missing direction virtues (the *what* and *why*). You could FDC your way to a billion dollars and a broken family. Add wisdom/discernment to know what deserves focus.

5. **On the stimulant cycle**: This is unsustainable. Sprint/crash is not Focus, Discipline, Consistency - it's intensity followed by depletion. Borrowing from health, presence as a father, and marriage to fund work sprints. Wife is right to flag it.
